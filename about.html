About Me

I'm quite interested in the following things:

  • Mathematics, especially branches with a geometric flavor.
  • Computer Science theory, especially computability, PL theory, type theory, and machine learning.
  • Semiotics, primarily applied towards industrial design. (One might infer my interest in colors from this page.)
  • Jazz, especially on piano (e.g., Thelonious Monk, Art Tatum) and synthesizer (e.g., Sun Ra).

I have formal training in the first two, but the rest are hobbies (though I am not entirely incompetent at the piano).

I sometimes do a bit of consulting, especially in regards to topics that are both mathematical and informatic in nature. Nowadays, I work primarily with machine learning frameworks.

I strongly believe that a good developer should have mastery with at least one language in each broad category. To that end, mine are the following (F means "fluent", IP means "in progress of learning"):

  • Low-level, statically typed, imperative, general purpose: C++ (F), C (F);
  • ML-style functional: OCaml (F), Haskell (F);
  • Lisp-style functional: Clojure (IP);
  • High-level, dynamically typed, general purpose: Python (F), Ruby (IP);
  • Mathematical and statistical programming: R (IP); MATLAB/Octave (F);
  • Web frontend: Javascript (F), HTML/CSS (F)
  • Concurrent: Erlang (IP)
